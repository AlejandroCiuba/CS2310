About Me

My name is Alejandro Ciuba (pronounced see-you-bah). I am a third year PhD student in computer science at the University of Pittsburgh. My main research focus is the intersection of language technologies and human-computer interaction. I enjoy making music and art.

Projects

Not Just Duolingo: Supporting Immigrant Families Through Family-Based Play

This ongoing project seeks to support immigrant families looking to pass on their language to their children. We are developing a point-and-click audio-focused game in the Godot game engine which encourages parent-child interaction and auditory language learning. Through this project, I have learned more about game design, using the Godot game engine, and leading a research team.

Graduate Student Researcher Attitudes on LLM Usage

This ongoing project explores how graduate student researchers navigate LLMs in their research work. Through both a distributed online survey via Qualtrics and Zoom semi-structured interviews, we articulate how graduate student researchers feel about LLM usage in academia, the ways they utilize or resist LLMs in their own work, and how we can consider their viewpoints when designing future technologies. This project has taught me how to run surveys using Qualtrics, how to send mass emails, how to organize participant data, and how to coordinate with collaborators.
